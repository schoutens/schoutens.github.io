Instructions

This is a asynchronous course, which means that we will not have any real-time meetings. All course material consists of videos and will be listed on this web site. Since there is no real-time nor real-live oversight, you alone are responsible for timing and scheduling your class work.

Honor Code

    Before we start, I expect everybody to pledge their commitment to the online format and to honor the rules of conduct; see in particular below for the rules for quizzes. To this end, I want each of you to read the rules of conduct (including the footnotes), and then handwrite (!) the following phrase, sign and date it, and then deposit in your own Dropbox folder for this course.

    I, [your name], pledge to uphold all rules of our honor code: [your signature and date]

    I will not correct any of your quizzes until I have obtained your pledge. VERY IMPORTANT:Do this right after you read all the introductory material; it is a way for me to test that our shared Dropfolder works properly. In these times of uncertainty, trust in each other and our social institutions is even more important than ever, so do not take this lightly. Be a man/woman of your word. You would want to earn this degree in a fair manner, as you would your future colleagues or employees. Here is a copy of my pledge.

Lectures:

you are assumed to be watching the lecture video(s) on the regular scheduled days. The topic and the material for each lecture will be listed on this website. Each lecture consists of one or more YouTube videos posted on my Differential Equations channel. The very first time you open one of them, there is a red button just below it asking for you to SUBSCRIBE to the channel. Login if necessary and click on that. Everyone has to be subscribed to this channel!!! You can also ask to be notified about new updates by clicking on the bell, but that is entirely up to you. These videos will be posted the morning of our  class time, or earlier. You are expected to have viewed them by the end of that day. VERY IMPORTANT: To let me know that you have seen the video, you have to add a comment containing your name. This is very important and will take the role of attendance, so please do not forget this. And make sure that I know who you are (your handle may be too obscure, so please enter your name). You can of course add more comments and ask questions. I will try to respond to them as quickly as possible, especially when posted during the office hours (see below). The comment section does not allow pictures, and so if you need to include some math, it is better to ask me through email.

Homeworks:

You're expected to do all homework by the beginning of next class. I will neither collect nor grade HW, but I will typically assign, from each section, two special problems which I will solve the next session. The way you earn points for making your HW is by every time after you did your HW and watched the HW solution video, to self-assess your progress by giving yourself one to four stars (on the BB web site; see HW self-assessment on the left menu). I will not look at your self-grade, it is entirely for your own benefit. Just grading yourself will give you credit for that particular HW assignment. It is important to do the HW regularly and preferably before the next class, but I will allow late submissions until the end of the week. Any self-assessment of the HW after the weekend may result in a lesser grade (you can be late once or so, but not regularly!). Since HW is intended for you to learn you can seek outside help, or collaborate with your peers. Just be honest when you self-assess.

Quizzes:

There will be daily quizzes. Each quiz consists typically of one question, unless the problems are short. The lowest quiz score will be dropped. As they make out 70% of your grade, these quizzes are therefore extremely important! Each quiz will have a review video posted in the previous session. The quiz itself will be available on the day of the class; you will have time until the end of the day to submit the quiz. Submission is always via the personal Dropbox folder that I shared with you. Since I have no direct way of verifying that you have done the problem on your own, the honor system is crucial. Be aware though: there are many indirect ways to verify academic integrity, and I have found, regrettably, in the past that some students think they can cover up their dishonesty!

The rules for quizzes and final:

  • you are allowed to use notes and videos and the textbook to solve the problem and use your (graphing) calculator (in other words, we use so-called `open book' testing).
  • you will not get much credit for the answer even if it is correct! Most of the credit will come from your work, and any missing work/steps will be penalized. This is basically the only way I have to verify that you are the author of the work.
  • you are NOT allowed to use any online tools such as Wolfram-Alpha or online help to solve the problem!
  • you are NOT allowed to ask anybody else's help, the work must be entirely yours!
